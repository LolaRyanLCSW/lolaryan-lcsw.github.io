Burnout & Caring Professionals

Caring professionals tend to be good at noticing when other people are struggling and not so good at noticing when they are. By the time most people come to therapy for burnout, they’ve been running on fumes for a while and have gotten used to it.

This work isn’t about learning to care less. It’s about understanding why giving to others has felt easier than attending to yourself, and building something more sustainable on the other side of that.

We’ll work at a pace that makes sense for you. Sessions are via telehealth throughout Oregon.

College Students & Life Transitions

College and the years around it can be genuinely disorienting, even for people who look like they have it together. You might be navigating new independence, shifting relationships, questions about identity or direction, or pressure from multiple directions at once.

Good therapy during this period isn’t about fixing something broken. It’s about having a consistent space to think clearly and figure out what you actually want, with someone who isn’t going to tell you what that should be.

Sessions are via telehealth throughout Oregon.

Immigrant Communities & Multicultural Therapy

There are things that come with building a life in a new country that are genuinely hard to explain to people who haven’t done it. The work of navigating systems that weren’t designed for you, holding multiple languages and identities at once, grieving things that don’t have an easy name.

I’m an immigrant myself, and I was trained at OHSU’s Intercultural Psychiatric Program specifically to work with people navigating these experiences. That combination shapes how I work.

Sessions are available in English, Russian, Tajik, and Uzbek. You don’t have to translate yourself to be understood here.
