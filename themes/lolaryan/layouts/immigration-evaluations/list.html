Immigration Psychological Evaluations

An immigration psychological evaluation is a clinical assessment conducted by a licensed mental health professional and submitted as evidence in immigration proceedings. The resulting report documents a client's psychological history, current mental health status, and — depending on the case type — the psychological impact of deportation, family separation, or return to a country of origin.

This is work Lola pursues deliberately. As an immigrant and a clinician trained in intercultural mental health, she brings both professional expertise and genuine understanding of what her clients are navigating.

Background & Qualifications

Lola Ryan is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker in Oregon with clinical training from the Intercultural Psychiatric Program at Oregon Health & Science University (OHSU). Her background includes work with the UN Population Fund in Uzbekistan, where she worked directly with communities affected by displacement and cross-cultural mental health challenges.

She is herself an immigrant. This matters clinically: clients recounting trauma, persecution, or the weight of acculturation are speaking to someone who understands that experience from the inside — not just as a clinician, but as a person.

Lola conducts evaluations in English and Russian. For Russian-speaking clients, this means the clinical interview is conducted in the client's native language without a translator, producing a more complete and credible record. She has working knowledge of Tajik and Uzbek.

Her reports are written to be clearly understood by immigration judges, attorneys, and USCIS adjudicators, and are prepared to professional forensic standards.

Evaluations Offered

Extreme Hardship (I-601 / I-601A Waivers)

Documenting the psychological impact on a qualifying U.S. citizen or permanent resident family member if their loved one is deported or denied entry.

Cancellation of Removal

Assessing hardship to qualifying family members in removal proceedings, supporting claims for cancellation under INA § 240A.

Asylum Evaluations

Documenting trauma history, PTSD, anxiety, depression, or other psychological conditions consistent with the client's account of persecution or well-founded fear of return. Supports credibility and corroborates the basis for asylum claims.

VAWA Evaluations

Assessing psychological harm experienced by survivors of abuse by a U.S. citizen or permanent resident spouse or parent. Supports self-petition under the Violence Against Women Act.

U Visa Evaluations

Documenting psychological harm experienced by victims of qualifying crimes who have cooperated with law enforcement.

T Visa Evaluations

Documenting psychological harm experienced by survivors of human trafficking.

Special Immigrant Juvenile Status (SIJS)

Assessing the best interests and psychological welfare of juvenile immigrants who have been abused, neglected, or abandoned, in support of SIJS findings.
