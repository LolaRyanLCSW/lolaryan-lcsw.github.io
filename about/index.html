I'm Lola Ryan, a Licensed Clinical Social Worker based in Oregon. I work with caring professionals experiencing burnout, college students navigating transitions, and immigrant communities — including Russian-speaking clients — who are building lives across cultures.

I came to this work through my own experience. As an immigrant, a professional, and a mother of young children, I know firsthand what it means to carry a lot at once — to feel the pull between responsibility and your own needs, between the world you came from and the one you're building.

Before private practice, I trained at OHSU's Intercultural Psychiatric Program and worked with the UN Population Fund in Uzbekistan. I bring that international and cross-cultural experience into every client relationship.

How We Work Together

We'll take time to build trust before we approach anything sensitive. Therapy isn't linear — sometimes we move forward, sometimes we take a step back or sideways, and that's okay. I draw on your internal wisdom to help resolve what's troubling you, using practices tailored to your feedback and needs.

I believe you already hold much of what you need. My role is to help you access it.

Training & Background

I hold a Master of Social Work degree and am a Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW) in the state of Oregon. My clinical training includes the Intercultural Psychiatric Program at Oregon Health & Science University (OHSU), with a focus on multicultural approaches to mental health.

I speak English, Russian, Tajik, and Uzbek, and I have experience working with international organizations including the UN Population Fund. I am credentialed with Care Oregon and accept Oregon Health Plan (OHP).
