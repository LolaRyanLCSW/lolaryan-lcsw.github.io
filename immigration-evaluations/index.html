Immigration Psychological Evaluations

Clinical evaluations and forensic reports for immigration attorneys and their clients in Oregon.

What Is an Immigration Psychological Evaluation?

An immigration psychological evaluation is a formal clinical assessment conducted by a licensed mental health professional. The evaluation produces a written report documenting a client's psychological history, current mental health status, and — depending on the case type — the potential psychological impact of deportation, separation from family, or return to a country of origin.

These reports are submitted as supporting evidence in immigration proceedings. Courts, immigration judges, and USCIS adjudicators use them to better understand the human impact of their decisions. A well-prepared evaluation can be a meaningful part of a case.

Evaluations Offered

Extreme Hardship Evaluations

Documenting the psychological impact on a qualifying U.S. citizen or permanent resident family member if their loved one is deported or denied entry. Used in I-601 and I-601A waiver applications.

VAWA Evaluations

Assessing psychological harm experienced by survivors of abuse by a U.S. citizen or permanent resident spouse or parent. Supports self-petition under the Violence Against Women Act.

Asylum Evaluations

Documenting trauma history, PTSD, anxiety, depression, or other psychological conditions consistent with the client's account of persecution or fear of return. Corroborates credibility and supports asylum claims.

U Visa & T Visa Evaluations

Documenting psychological harm experienced by victims of qualifying crimes (U visa) or human trafficking (T visa). Supports certification and application.

How the Process Works

1

Attorney Referral & Intake

The referring attorney contacts Lola directly to discuss the case type and timeline. A brief intake is scheduled with the client to confirm fit and availability. Fees and turnaround time are discussed at this stage.

2

Clinical Assessment

The evaluation consists of one or more clinical interviews with the client, review of relevant case documents provided by the attorney, and administration of standardized psychological measures where appropriate. Sessions are conducted via telehealth.

3

Report Preparation & Delivery

A comprehensive written report is prepared documenting clinical findings, relevant history, and conclusions. The report is delivered to the referring attorney in a format suitable for submission. Lola is available to answer follow-up questions from the attorney.
